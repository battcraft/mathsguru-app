Spherical Geometry

On a flat plane, the shortest distance between two points is a straight line. On a sphere, it's a curve called a Geodesic. Parallel lines eventually intersect!

Orbital Mechanics

Satellites don't fly in 'straight' lines; they fall continuously around the curve of the Earth, following a geodesic path shaped by gravity.

The Geodesic Principle

Whether determining airplane flight paths or calculating satellite trajectories, understanding non-Euclidean lines is essential. The "Great Circle" path is the mathematically proven shortest route across our planet's surface.
